# Technical SEO Audit Report -- orangejelly.co.uk

**Date:** 2026-03-23
**Auditor:** Technical SEO Specialist Agent
**Status:** Phase 2 -- Discovery
**Site:** https://www.orangejelly.co.uk
**Stack:** Next.js App Router on Vercel, ISR (60s revalidation), static blog from Markdown

---

## Executive Summary

Only ~4 of 103 indexable pages appear in Google's index. The primary root cause is **not a technical blocking issue** -- robots.txt allows crawling, the sitemap is valid, and pages render correctly with proper canonical tags. The indexation failure is caused by a combination of: (1) a young domain with minimal backlink authority, (2) zero internal links from the homepage to 90%+ of content, (3) a flat-but-disconnected site architecture with orphan-like pages, and (4) sitemap `lastModified` dates that are misleading. Google has no reason to prioritise crawling 103 pages on a domain it barely knows, especially when the homepage does not link to them.

**Critical finding count:** 4 critical, 8 high, 11 medium, 6 low.

## Critical Issues (Fix Immediately)

### CRIT-1: Homepage Links to Zero Blog Posts and Zero Location Pages

**What:** The homepage contains exactly 1 link to `/licensees-guide` (the blog index), 1 link to `/pub-marketing` (pillar page), and 1 link to `/quiet-midweek-solutions`. It contains zero links to any of the 71 blog posts, zero links to any of the 8 location pages, and zero links to `/pub-rescue`, `/empty-pub-solutions`, `/fix-my-pub`, `/compete-with-pub-chains`, or `/pub-marketing-no-budget`.

**Why it matters:** The homepage is the highest-authority page on the site. Google discovers and prioritises pages based on internal link signals. Pages that are only reachable via the sitemap (not linked from the site) receive minimal crawl priority. For a new, low-authority domain, this is the single biggest reason Google has not indexed most pages.

**How to fix:**
1. Add a "Latest Articles" section to the homepage linking to 6-9 recent blog posts
2. Add a "We Cover Your Area" section linking to all 8 location pages
3. Add a "Common Problems We Solve" section linking to the solution pages (pub-rescue, empty-pub-solutions, fix-my-pub, etc.)

**Estimated impact:** HIGH -- this alone could double or triple the indexed page count within 4-6 weeks.

### CRIT-2: Location Pages Are Orphaned from Each Other

**What:** The 8 location pages (pub-marketing-surrey, pub-marketing-london, etc.) do not link to each other. Each location page contains only navigation links. The homepage does not link to any of them. The pillar page `/pub-marketing` does not appear to link to them either.

**Why it matters:** Google treats unlinked pages as low-importance. These pages form a natural cluster that should cross-reference each other ("Also serving: Surrey, London, Berkshire..."). Without cross-links, Google has no structural signal that these pages are related or important.

**How to fix:**
1. Add a "We also serve" or "Other areas" section to each location page linking to all other location pages
2. Add location links from the `/pub-marketing` pillar page
3. Add location links from the homepage

**Estimated impact:** HIGH -- location pages should start appearing in local search results within 4-8 weeks.

### CRIT-3: Naked Domain Redirect Uses 307 (Temporary) Instead of 301 (Permanent)

**What:** `https://orangejelly.co.uk` redirects to `https://www.orangejelly.co.uk` via a 307 Temporary Redirect. The HTTP version (`http://orangejelly.co.uk`) uses a 308 Permanent Redirect to HTTPS, but then the HTTPS naked domain uses a 307.

**Redirect chain observed:**
```
http://orangejelly.co.uk -> 308 -> https://orangejelly.co.uk -> 307 -> https://www.orangejelly.co.uk
```

**Why it matters:** A 307 tells Google that the redirect is temporary and not to transfer link equity permanently. Any backlinks pointing to `orangejelly.co.uk` (without www) are having their PageRank diluted. The middleware code at line 113 correctly uses `301`, but Vercel's edge infrastructure appears to be overriding this with a 307. This needs investigation in Vercel's project settings (Domains configuration).

**How to fix:**
1. Check Vercel Dashboard > Project Settings > Domains -- ensure `orangejelly.co.uk` is configured to redirect to `www.orangejelly.co.uk` with a 301/308 permanent redirect
2. Verify the middleware redirect at `src/middleware.ts` line 113 is being reached (the Vercel edge may be handling this before middleware runs)

**Estimated impact:** MEDIUM -- ensures any backlinks to the naked domain pass full authority.

### CRIT-4: Sitemap lastModified Dates Are Misleading for All Static/Marketing Pages

**What:** All 39 static and marketing pages in the sitemap have the identical `lastModified` timestamp of `2026-03-04T17:59:43.969Z` (the last build time). This is generated by `new Date().toISOString()` in `src/app/sitemap.ts` line 8.

**Why it matters:** Google uses lastModified as a signal for crawl priority. When every page claims to have been updated at the same time on every deployment, Google learns to distrust the signal and may reduce crawl frequency. This is confirmed as a known anti-pattern in Google's sitemap documentation.

**How to fix:**
1. Replace `new Date().toISOString()` with actual content modification dates
2. For static pages, hardcode realistic dates based on when the content was last meaningfully changed
3. For marketing/landing pages, use a content hash or git commit date approach
4. Blog posts already use real dates from frontmatter -- this is correct

**Estimated impact:** MEDIUM -- improves crawl efficiency and Google's trust in the sitemap signal.

## Summary of Root Causes for Low Indexation

The site is technically sound in terms of crawlability signals (robots.txt, sitemap, canonical tags, meta robots). Google **can** find and render these pages. The problem is that Google is **choosing not to prioritise** crawling and indexing them. Here is why:

1. **No internal link authority flows to most content.** The homepage -- the only page with any external authority -- links to 6 navigation pages and 2 content pages. The remaining 95 pages receive zero internal link equity from the homepage.

2. **Domain authority is near zero.** The site is ~6 months into active commercial use with a thin backlink profile. Google allocates crawl budget proportional to perceived site quality. A new domain gets a minimal crawl budget.

3. **The sitemap is the only discovery mechanism for most pages.** Without strong internal linking, Google relies on the sitemap alone to find pages. For a low-authority domain, sitemap-only discovery means slow, incremental indexation.

4. **Misleading lastModified dates** reduce Google's trust in the sitemap's freshness signals, potentially causing Google to deprioritise recrawling.

5. **The 307 redirect** from the naked domain may be causing Google to see the www and non-www versions as separate entities, diluting any small amount of authority the domain has.

**Bottom line:** Fix internal linking first (items 1-4, 6 in the priority list). This is the highest-leverage action. No amount of schema or image optimization will fix indexation if Google cannot find the pages through internal links. Once internal linking is fixed, submit the updated sitemap to Google Search Console and request indexing of the key pages. Expect to see meaningful indexation improvements within 4-8 weeks.
