## 6. Anchor Text Strategy

### Recommended Anchor Text Distribution

To avoid over-optimisation penalties, backlinks should use varied anchor text:

| Anchor Text Type | Target % | Examples |
|-----------------|----------|---------|
| Brand name | 40% | "Orange Jelly", "Orange Jelly pub marketing" |
| URL / naked link | 20% | "orangejelly.co.uk", "www.orangejelly.co.uk" |
| Generic / natural | 20% | "click here", "this guide", "read more", "their website" |
| Descriptive | 15% | "pub marketing consultancy", "AI marketing for pubs", "pub marketing advice" |
| Exact match keyword | 5% max | "pub marketing agency" (use sparingly) |

### Anchor Texts to Avoid

- Exact match commercial keywords as primary anchor: "pub marketing agency", "pub marketing consultant"
- Repetitive identical anchors across multiple sites
- Keyword-stuffed anchors: "best pub marketing agency UK Surrey London"

## 7. Link Quality Guidelines

### What Makes a Good Backlink for Orange Jelly

| Quality Signal | Why It Matters |
|---------------|---------------|
| Topically relevant | Link from a pub, hospitality, or marketing site carries more weight than a random blog |
| Editorially placed | Links within article body > footer links > sidebar links |
| High DR source | Morning Advertiser (DR 60-70) > random blog (DR 5) |
| Followed link | dofollow links pass PageRank; nofollow links still provide brand signals |
| Unique referring domain | 10 links from 10 different sites > 10 links from 1 site |
| UK-based | UK domains strengthen UK geo-relevance signals |
| Natural context | Link appears because the content genuinely references Orange Jelly's expertise |

### Red Flags to Avoid

- Sites that sell links openly
- Sites with no real traffic or content
- Irrelevant sites (e.g., technology blogs with no hospitality connection)
- Sites with thin, AI-generated content farms
- Reciprocal link exchanges at scale
- Footer or sidebar-only link placements
- Links from sites in different languages or countries (unless genuinely relevant)
