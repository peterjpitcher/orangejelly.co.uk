## 7. Risk Assessment

### Link Building Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|----------|------------|
| Trade press pitches rejected | Medium | Low | Diversify pitch angles; offer exclusives; lead with data not promotion |
| Awards not shortlisted | Medium | Low | Enter multiple awards; treat as PR exercise regardless of outcome |
| HARO/ResponseSource yields few relevant queries | Medium | Low | Cast wide net across hospitality, small business, AI categories |
| Competitor copies linkable assets | Low | Low | First-mover advantage; iterate and improve; brand the tools |
| Google algorithm penalises low-quality directory links | Very Low | Medium | Only submit to reputable directories; avoid PBNs and link farms |
| Brand name confusion with food products ("Orange Jelly") | High | Medium | Always use full brand "Orange Jelly pub marketing" in anchor text; build branded search volume |

### What NOT To Do

1. **Do not buy links.** Google's spam policies are well-enforced in the UK market. Any short-term gain will result in long-term penalty risk.
2. **Do not use link exchange schemes.** "I'll link to you if you link to me" at scale is a known spam signal.
3. **Do not use PBN (Private Blog Network) links.** These are the highest-risk link type in 2026.
4. **Do not submit to hundreds of low-quality directories.** Focus on 20-30 reputable, relevant directories only.
5. **Do not use automated outreach tools for link requests.** Personal, relationship-based outreach only.
6. **Do not create thin "guest posts" on irrelevant sites.** Every piece of content should provide genuine value to the target publication's audience.
7. **Do not over-optimise anchor text.** Use natural, varied anchor text -- brand name, URL, descriptive phrases. Avoid exact-match keyword anchors (e.g., "pub marketing agency") which trigger Google's Penguin filter.
