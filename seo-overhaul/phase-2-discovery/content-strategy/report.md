## Executive Summary

Orange Jelly has 72 blog posts (66 published, 6 draft), 8 location pages, 6 solution/landing pages, 5 social media service pages, a pillar page, and an agency comparison page. This represents one of the deepest pub-marketing-specific content libraries of any UK consultancy. However, only ~4 pages are indexed by Google, meaning 96% of this content investment is currently invisible to search.

This report maps the full content inventory, identifies gaps and cannibalisation issues, assesses the SERP landscape, and provides a prioritised action plan with content briefs for the highest-impact new pieces.

**Key findings:**
1. 6 draft posts need immediate publishing -- they cover keywords with zero alternative coverage
2. Only 8 of 72 blog posts have SEO title/description overrides
3. 3 solution pages (fix-my-pub, empty-pub-solutions, pub-rescue) cannibalise each other
4. 10+ high-value keyword gaps exist where no content covers the search intent
5. Orange Jelly already ranks on page 1 for "how to compete with wetherspoons" and appears for "quiz night ideas" -- proving the content model works
6. No competitor has Orange Jelly's depth of pub-specific content, location pages, or crisis-intent content

## 3. Cannibalisation Analysis

### 3.1 Critical: Crisis/Empty Pub Pages (HIGH RISK)

Three pages compete for overlapping keywords:

| Page | Target Keywords | Unique Angle |
|------|----------------|-------------|
| /fix-my-pub | "fix my pub" | Quick diagnostic, CTA-focused |
| /empty-pub-solutions | "empty pub solutions", "my pub is empty" | Detailed 30-day plan |
| /pub-rescue | "pub rescue", "struggling pub" | Emergency turnaround |

**Problem:** Similar content structure (diagnosis + plan + Anchor results + WhatsApp CTA). Google cannot determine which to rank.

**Recommendation:**
- **Keep /fix-my-pub** as the primary conversion page (short, diagnostic, strong CTA)
- **Keep /empty-pub-solutions** as the detailed informational page (long-form, 30-day plan, FAQ schema)
- **Redirect /pub-rescue to /fix-my-pub** OR completely rewrite it with genuinely different content (e.g., "Pub Rescue: The 7-Day Emergency Turnaround" -- shorter timeline, more urgent tone, different structure)
- Add canonical tag from /pub-rescue to /fix-my-pub if not redirecting

### 3.2 Moderate: Blog Category vs Landing Page

- Category URL: `/licensees-guide/category/empty-pub-solutions` (aggregates 9 posts)
- Landing page: `/empty-pub-solutions`

Both pages appear for "empty pub solutions" queries.

**Recommendation:** Add `noindex` to category pages OR set canonical from category pages to the corresponding landing page. Category pages should serve navigation, not compete in SERPs.

### 3.3 Moderate: Social Media Content Overlap

Blog posts covering social media:
- social-media-strategy-for-pubs (comprehensive strategy)
- social-media-tactics-footfall-seven-days (tactical, 7-day focus)
- facebook-marketing-local-pubs (Facebook-specific)
- instagram-marketing-for-pubs (Instagram-specific)
- content-marketing-ideas-pubs (content ideas)

Service pages:
- /services/social-media-marketing-for-pubs
- /services/facebook-services-for-pubs
- /services/instagram-services-for-pubs
- /services/content-creation-for-pubs

**Recommendation:** This is manageable with clear intent differentiation:
- Blog posts = informational ("how to do X yourself")
- Service pages = commercial ("hire us to do X")
- Every blog post must link to its corresponding service page
- Every service page must link to its supporting blog posts

### 3.4 Low: Quiz Night Content

- quiz-night-101 (setup guide -- "how to run")
- quiz-night-ideas (format ideas -- "what to do")
- restart-quiz-music-sport-roi (restart guide -- "getting going again")

**Recommendation:** Differentiate clearly in SEO titles:
- quiz-night-101: "How to Run a Pub Quiz Night (Complete Setup Guide)" [already has override]
- quiz-night-ideas: "25 Quiz Night Ideas & Formats That Pack Your Pub Every Week"
- restart-quiz-music-sport-roi: "How to Restart Quiz, Music & Sport Nights for Maximum ROI"

### 3.5 Low: Reviews/Reputation Content

- crisis-pr-landlords-bad-reviews (published -- crisis response)
- terrible-online-reviews-damage-control (draft -- damage control)

**Recommendation:** These have distinct angles. Publish the draft but ensure titles clearly differentiate:
- crisis-pr: "Crisis PR for Pub Landlords: Turning Bad Reviews into Bookings"

### 6.2 Blog Post Internal Link Template

Every blog post should contain at minimum:
1. One link to /pub-marketing (pillar) in the introduction or first section
2. One link to the most relevant service/solution page in the body
3. Two to three links to related blog posts in the body
4. One CTA link to /fix-my-pub or /contact in the closing section

### 6.3 Related Posts Section

The codebase has `getRelatedPosts()` functionality. Ensure this renders visibly on every blog post page with 3-4 related articles, improving internal linking, reducing bounce rate, and signalling topical clusters.
