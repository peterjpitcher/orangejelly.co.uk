# Competitor Landscape -- Orange Jelly (orangejelly.co.uk)

**Date:** 2026-03-23
**Status:** Phase 1 -- Strategy & Research

---

## Competitor Classification

Orange Jelly operates in a niche intersection: pub-specific marketing consulting. Competitors fall into four categories:

1. **Hospitality marketing agencies** (Brew Digital, YesMore, SideDish, Toast) -- full-service agencies with pub clients
2. **Pub consultancy firms** (HowToRunAPub, Hotel & Pub Rescue, Bowden Group) -- operational consultants, not marketing-focused
3. **Pub marketing content sites** (SmartPubTools, Premierline blog) -- content/tool providers, not consultancies
4. **General hospitality content** (Morning Advertiser, Caterer Licensee & Hotelier) -- trade press

---

## Competitor 1: Brew Digital (wearebrew.com)

### Profile
- **Type:** Full-service hospitality digital marketing agency
- **Location:** Birmingham (STEAMhouse)
- **Founded:** ~2008 (16+ years in hospitality)
- **Size:** Medium agency team
- **Clients:** Pub groups, restaurant chains, hospitality brands UK-wide
- **Awards:** Digital Marketing Agency of the Year 2025 (We Are Creative Awards), Midlands Marketing Awards finalist

### Services
- Branding & design
- Web development & hosting (React, WordPress)
- SEO
- Google PPC / paid advertising
- Social media & content creation
- Meta Partner Agency status

### Content & SEO
- **Blog:** Active pub marketing blog with "Pub Marketing Tips for 2025", "Local Pub Marketing Tips", "2026 Marketing Dates in Hospitality", "Mastering Local SEO for Hospitality"
- **Annual report:** "Pints & Profits" pub industry report -- excellent linkable asset
- **Content depth:** Estimated 15-30 pub-specific blog posts, plus broader hospitality content
- **SEO strength:** Very strong domain authority from 16 years of links, client portfolio pages, industry awards

### Where They Win
- Domain authority -- years of link building and brand recognition
- Breadth of services -- full digital suite from web to PPC to social
- "Pints & Profits" report -- generates press coverage and backlinks annually
- Enterprise clients -- work with pub groups, not just independents
- Meta Partner status -- credibility signal for paid social

### Where They're Vulnerable
- **Price:** Agency pricing (likely GBP 1,500-5,000/month retainer) excludes struggling independents
- **Generalist:** Serve all hospitality, not pub-specific -- content breadth but not depth
- **No practitioner credibility:** Nobody at Brew runs a pub. They are marketers advising publicans. Orange Jelly has E-E-A-T advantage here
- **Blog depth:** ~15-30 pub posts vs Orange Jelly's 61. Less topical authority in the narrow "pub marketing advice" niche
- **No location pages:** No "pub marketing [county]" content
- **No crisis content:** No "fix my pub" or "pub rescue" style content targeting distressed licensees

### What Orange Jelly Can Learn
- Create a downloadable report/guide as a linkable asset (equivalent to "Pints & Profits")
- Develop clear case studies with before/after metrics (Brew shows client logos but OJ can show depth)
- Consider a webinar or video series (Brew does digital marketing trends webinars)

- **Founded:** Recent (2024-2025 era)

- **Model:** SaaS subscription (not consulting)

- Quiz question generation
- Marketing psychology-based content templates

- **Content quality:** Well-written, practical, pub-specific
- **AI angle:** Strong positioning around "AI for pubs" -- overlaps with Orange Jelly's AI narrative

### Where They Win
- **Direct licensee credibility:** Shaun runs a pub, same as Peter -- genuine E-E-A-T
- **SaaS tool:** A product that delivers ongoing value vs. hourly consulting
- **AI positioning:** Clearly positioned as "AI for pubs" -- a growing search term
- **Content marketing:** Blog posts are SEO-optimised and targeting the same long-tail keywords as OJ

### Where They're Vulnerable
- **Small content library:** ~10-20 posts vs Orange Jelly's 61
- **No location pages:** No geographic targeting
- **No consulting depth:** Tool, not a consultant -- cannot help with strategy, menu engineering, event design
- **Narrow offering:** Only social media content creation, not full marketing consulting
- **SSL certificate issues:** Site had SSL errors during research, suggesting technical instability
- **No proven results page:** No equivalent to Orange Jelly's results/case studies

### What Orange Jelly Can Learn
- The "AI for pubs" keyword cluster is a genuine opportunity that OJ should own (given BII AI innovation feature)
- A simple tool or calculator (drinks margin calculator, quiz ROI calculator) could be a powerful differentiator
- SmartPubTools proves that the licensee-to-licensee content model works for SEO

## Competitor 3: Hotel & Pub Rescue (hotelandpubrescue.co.uk)

### Profile
- **Type:** Hospitality business rescue and operational consultancy
- **Location:** National (UK)
- **Founded:** Established
- **Size:** Small-medium team with management consultants
- **Pricing:** GBP 15/hour for general consultation (much cheaper than OJ), higher for operational services

### Services
- Business rescue and turnaround
- Contract & relief management (temporary managers, chefs)
- Full accounting & payroll management
- Procurement & discounted brewery products
- Stocktaking
- Company setup & business structuring
- Sell your business support

### Content & SEO
- **Content:** Service pages, limited blog content
- **SEO focus:** Targets "pub rescue", "hotel rescue", "hospitality business rescue"
- **Domain authority:** Moderate, benefits from trade press coverage (CLH News feature)

### Where They Win
- **"Pub rescue" keyword:** Directly targets the crisis-intent search
- **Operational breadth:** Can provide temporary staff, accounting, procurement -- things OJ cannot
- **Lower entry price:** GBP 15/hour consultation undercuts OJ significantly for initial conversations
- **Insolvency-adjacent:** Can help with formal business rescue procedures

### Where They're Vulnerable
- **Not marketing-focused:** They rescue businesses operationally, not through marketing and growth
- **No content strategy:** Minimal blog presence -- no informational content capturing top-of-funnel
- **Different audience:** Their ideal client is in financial distress and considering closure. OJ's ideal client is struggling but wants to grow
- **No digital marketing capability:** Cannot help with social media, Google, events marketing

### What Orange Jelly Can Learn
- The GBP 15/hour initial consultation model is compelling. OJ could offer a free 15-minute diagnostic call (already somewhat positioned via WhatsApp CTA)
- Clearly differentiate: OJ = marketing growth partner. H&PR = operational rescue. Different situations, different solutions
- Consider referral partnership: when H&PR clients need marketing help, refer to OJ and vice versa

## Differentiation Opportunities

### 1. The Licensee Advantage
No large agency can replicate "I run a pub and built this at my own bar." This is Orange Jelly's permanent moat. Every piece of content should reference specific numbers from The Anchor. Every recommendation should start with "we tried this and..."

**Action:** Create a dedicated author/about page for Peter that emphasises operational credentials. Link to BII feature. Add structured data for Person entity.

### 2. Content Depth in the Narrow Niche
With 61 posts (and growing), Orange Jelly already has the deepest pub-marketing-specific content library of any consultant. Brew has breadth across hospitality but not depth in pubs. SmartPubTools is catching up but is currently smaller.

**Action:** Maintain weekly publishing cadence. Target the 25 keyword gaps identified in the keyword framework. Build the internal linking architecture that signals topical authority to Google.

### 3. Crisis Funnel Ownership
No competitor specifically targets distressed licensees with marketing solutions. Hotel & Pub Rescue targets distressed licensees but for operational/financial rescue, not marketing growth. Orange Jelly can own the "my pub is struggling -- help me market it back to health" journey.

**Action:** Resolve cannibalisation between fix-my-pub, empty-pub-solutions, and pub-rescue. Build a clear conversion funnel: blog post (problem awareness) > solution page (consideration) > fix-my-pub (action).

### 4. Location Authority
Zero competitors have county-specific pub marketing pages. This is free SERP real estate.

**Action:** Strengthen the 8 location pages with unique local content (specific pub towns, local events, county-specific challenges), local business schema, and cross-links to the blog content.

### 5. AI Innovation Story
Orange Jelly's AI angle (BII magazine feature, 25 hours/week saved) is a genuine differentiator. SmartPubTools targets "AI for pubs" as a product; OJ targets it as a consultancy service.

**Action:** Create dedicated "AI for Pubs" content that positions OJ as the expert consultant (not tool vendor) for licensees wanting to use AI. This captures an emerging keyword cluster and leverages the BII credibility.

### 6. Price Transparency
Most agencies hide pricing behind "contact us" CTAs. OJ's GBP 75/hour is visible everywhere. This builds trust and pre-qualifies leads.

**Action:** Maintain pricing visibility. Consider creating comparison content: "Pub Marketing Agency vs DIY vs Orange Jelly" that explicitly positions the hourly model against retainer agencies.
