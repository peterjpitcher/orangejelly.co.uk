## 1. Critical: Indexation Gap

**The single biggest opportunity is getting indexed.** Google currently shows only 4 pages for site:orangejelly.co.uk despite 100+ indexable pages existing. This means ~96% of content is invisible to search.

**Likely causes (investigate in priority order):**
1. Thin or missing internal linking -- Google cannot discover pages it cannot crawl to via links
2. Low domain authority + no backlinks = Google is slow to crawl new pages
3. Possible sitemap not being submitted or processed in Google Search Console
4. New domain/content -- Google may simply not have gotten to all pages yet

**Action:** Submit sitemap in Google Search Console. Use URL Inspection tool on key pages. Check the Coverage report for any excluded pages. Force-index the 20 highest priority pages using the "Request Indexing" feature.

**Commercial impact:** Until this is fixed, zero organic traffic will come from 96% of the content investment.

## 4. Cannibalisation Risks

### Risk 1: Empty Pub / Struggling Pub Pages (HIGH RISK)

Three pages target overlapping keywords:
- `/empty-pub-solutions` -- "empty pub solutions", "quiet nights to consistent trade"
- `/fix-my-pub` -- "fix my pub", "pub struggling"
- `/pub-rescue` -- "pub rescue", "struggling pub help"

**Problem:** Google does not know which to rank. All three have similar content: diagnosis, 30-day plan, results from The Anchor, CTA to WhatsApp.

**Recommendation:** Pick ONE as the primary "struggling pub" landing page. Make the other two redirect or differentiate clearly:
- `/fix-my-pub` = quick diagnostic (keep as the entry point / conversion page)
- `/empty-pub-solutions` = the detailed 30-day plan (informational depth)
- `/pub-rescue` = redirect to `/fix-my-pub` OR differentiate as "emergency turnaround" with different content

### Risk 2: Blog Categories vs Landing Pages

Some blog category slugs overlap with landing page slugs:
- Category: `empty-pub-solutions` (10 posts) vs Landing page: `/empty-pub-solutions`
- Category URL: `/licensees-guide/category/empty-pub-solutions`

**Problem:** The category page and the landing page compete for similar terms.

**Recommendation:** Ensure category pages have noindex or canonical to the landing page. Category pages should serve as navigation aids, not ranking targets.

### Risk 3: Multiple Social Media Posts

Several blog posts cover social media for pubs:
- `social-media-strategy-for-pubs`
- `social-media-tactics-footfall-seven-days`
- `facebook-marketing-local-pubs`
- `instagram-marketing-for-pubs`
- `content-marketing-ideas-pubs`

Plus service pages:
- `/services/social-media-marketing-for-pubs`
- `/services/facebook-services-for-pubs`
- `/services/instagram-services-for-pubs`
- `/services/content-creation-for-pubs`

**Problem:** Potential dilution of authority across too many similar pages.

**Recommendation:** Clear hierarchy:
- `/services/social-media-marketing-for-pubs` = commercial page (target "pub social media agency/services")
- Blog posts = informational (target "how to" and "ideas" queries)
- Each blog post must link to the relevant service page as CTA
- Each service page must link to relevant blog posts as supporting content

### Risk 4: Quiz Night Content

- `/licensees-guide/quiz-night-101` (toolkit guide)
- `/licensees-guide/quiz-night-ideas` (format ideas)
- `/licensees-guide/restart-quiz-music-sport-roi` (restart guide)

**Problem:** Moderate overlap but different enough angles. Main risk is quiz-night-101 vs quiz-night-ideas competing for "pub quiz night" searches.

**Recommendation:** Differentiate clearly in titles:
- quiz-night-101 = "How to Run a Pub Quiz Night (Complete Setup Guide)"
- quiz-night-ideas = "25 Quiz Night Ideas & Formats to Pack Your Pub"
- Internal link between them with clear directional anchor text

## 5. Structural Opportunities

### 5.1 Internal Linking Architecture (CRITICAL)

**Current state:** Blog posts appear to have minimal cross-linking. The pillar page at `/pub-marketing` links to some content, but there is no systematic internal linking strategy.

**Opportunity:** Create a hub-and-spoke model:

```
/pub-marketing (hub)
  |-- /licensees-guide/social-media-strategy-for-pubs
  |-- /licensees-guide/quiz-night-ideas
  |-- /licensees-guide/fill-empty-pub-tables
  |-- /licensees-guide/profitable-pub-food-menu-ideas
  |-- /licensees-guide/low-budget-pub-marketing-ideas
  |-- ... (all 61 blog posts link back to pillar)

/services (hub)
  |-- /services/social-media-marketing-for-pubs
  |-- /services/instagram-services-for-pubs
  |-- /services/facebook-services-for-pubs
  |-- /services/paid-social-for-pubs
  |-- /services/content-creation-for-pubs

/empty-pub-solutions (hub for crisis cluster)
  |-- /licensees-guide/why-is-my-pub-empty
  |-- /licensees-guide/pub-empty-tuesday-nights
  |-- /licensees-guide/fill-empty-pub-tables
  |-- /licensees-guide/revenue-levers-struggling-pubs
  |-- /licensees-guide/30-day-action-plan-stabilise-hospitality
```

**Every blog post should contain:**
1. A link to the pillar page `/pub-marketing`
2. A link to the most relevant service/solution page
3. 2-3 links to related blog posts
4. A CTA linking to `/fix-my-pub` or `/contact`

### 5.2 Blog URL Structure

**Current:** `/licensees-guide/[slug]`

**Assessment:** This is fine for branding but "licensees-guide" is not a search term. It does not hurt SEO, but it also does not help. The bigger issue is that Google may see this as a flat structure rather than a topical one.

**Recommendation (longer term):** Consider adding category segments: `/licensees-guide/events/quiz-night-ideas`. This signals topical relevance to Google. However, this is a URL change that requires redirects and is LOWER priority than the indexation and internal linking fixes.

### 5.3 Related Posts Section

The codebase has a `getRelatedPosts()` function that finds posts by category and tags. This should be rendered as a visible "Related Articles" section at the bottom of every blog post to:
1. Increase internal linking
2. Reduce bounce rate
3. Increase pages per session
4. Signal topical clusters to Google

### 5.4 Breadcrumb Enhancement

Breadcrumb schema exists in the codebase (`BreadcrumbJsonLd`, `BreadcrumbSchema`). Verify this is rendered on every page type:
- Blog posts: Home > Licensee's Guide > [Category] > [Post Title]
- Service pages: Home > Services > [Service Name]
- Location pages: Home > Pub Marketing > [County]
- Solution pages: Home > [Solution Page]

## 6. Rich Result Opportunities

### 6.1 FAQ Rich Results (Highest Impact)

**Current state:** FAQSchema component exists and is used on `/pub-marketing` pillar page. Most blog posts have FAQ sections in their frontmatter but NOT all render the schema.

**Opportunity:** Add FAQ schema to every page that has FAQ content. This includes:
- All blog posts with `faqs` frontmatter (check each of the 61 posts)
- All solution pages (fix-my-pub, empty-pub-solutions, pub-rescue, quiet-midweek-solutions)
- The pub-marketing-agency page
- Location pages (add local FAQs: "How much does pub marketing cost in Surrey?")

**Expected impact:** FAQ rich results increase CTR by 20-30% and can push results higher in SERPs due to SERP real estate.

### 6.2 HowTo Rich Results

**Current state:** HowToSchema component exists but usage is unclear.

**Opportunity:** Many blog posts contain step-by-step content that qualifies for HowTo rich results:
- quiz-night-101, karaoke-night-101, music-bingo-101, cash-bingo-101 (all "101" guides)
- how-to-run-successful-pub-events
- 30-day-action-plan-stabilise-hospitality
- pub-event-template-profit-nights
- build-loyalty-scheme-fill-pub

### 6.3 Article Rich Results (BlogPosting)

**Current state:** `BlogPostingSchema` component exists and should be rendering on blog posts.

**Verification needed:** Ensure all blog posts include proper BlogPosting schema with:
- datePublished, dateModified
- author with @type Person (not just name string)
- publisher with @type Organization
- image (required for Google Discover eligibility)

### 6.4 Service Rich Results

**Current state:** ServiceSchema component exists.

**Opportunity:** Render on `/services` and individual service pages with pricing (GBP 75/hour).

### 6.5 Review / Testimonial Rich Results

**Current state:** No review schema visible.

**Opportunity:** If any testimonials or client reviews exist (or can be collected), add Review schema to the results page and service pages.

### 6.6 LocalBusiness Rich Results

**Current state:** LocalBusinessSchema component exists.

**Opportunity:** Render on each of the 8 location pages with county-specific areaServed. Also ensure the global schema includes geo coordinates for The Anchor.
