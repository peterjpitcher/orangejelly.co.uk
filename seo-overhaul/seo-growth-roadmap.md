## Executive Summary

Orange Jelly has built the deepest pub-marketing-specific content library of any UK consultancy (66 published blog posts, 8 location pages, 6 solution pages, a pillar page, and an agency comparison page), but 96% of it is invisible to Google -- only 4 pages are indexed. The root cause is not a technical blocking issue but a combination of zero internal linking from the homepage to most content, near-zero domain authority (DR 0-5), and misleading sitemap dates. The one page that IS properly ranking -- "quiz night ideas" at position 1 for a 2K-5K/mo keyword -- proves the content quality is strong enough to compete. The problem is infrastructure, not content.

The top three priorities are: (1) fix internal linking from the homepage to rescue 95+ orphaned pages, (2) set up analytics and Search Console to enable measurement, and (3) optimise the top 20 blog posts with SEO overrides and FAQ schema. The highest-impact work (Tier 1) requires only 12-18 development hours and should be completed this week. Conservative projections show 500+ monthly organic clicks within 3 months and GBP 5,000+/month organic revenue attribution within 12 months.

## Strategic Direction

### Business Goals and SEO Alignment

Orange Jelly is 6 months into commercial consulting (first external client September 2025), operating at GBP 75/hour plus VAT with no retainers. The SEO strategy must generate a steady pipeline of inbound leads from pub licensees who are searching for help -- replacing outbound effort with organic discovery.

**Business goal:** GBP 5,000+/month in organic revenue (approximately 13 consulting hours/month from organic leads) within 12 months.

**SEO alignment:** The content engine exists. The indexation infrastructure does not. Fix infrastructure first, then optimise, then expand.

### Where This Site Can Win

**1. Long-tail informational keywords (highest volume, lowest competition).** No competitor has 66 blog posts specifically about pub operations and marketing. Brew Digital has 15-30 posts; SmartPubTools has 10-20. The quiz night ranking at #1 validates this approach.

**2. Crisis-intent keywords (highest conversion, weakest SERPs).** "My pub is empty", "struggling pub", "pub rescue" -- the top results for these queries are VinePair interviews, property guardian sites, and Quora. No UK pub marketing consultancy has a dedicated result. OJ's solution pages would likely reach page 1 within weeks of proper indexation.

**3. Location keywords (zero competition).** No competitor has county-specific pub marketing pages. Eight location pages exist targeting Surrey, London, Berkshire, Buckinghamshire, Hampshire, Hertfordshire, Kent, and Oxfordshire -- all currently orphaned from the homepage.

**4. The licensee advantage (permanent moat).** Peter is a working Greene King tenant who runs The Anchor. Every competitor is either an agency (Brew Digital, YesMore) or a SaaS tool (SmartPubTools). This is an E-E-A-T advantage that Google explicitly rewards and no competitor can replicate.

## Key Findings by Discipline

### Technical SEO
**Overall health:** Good foundation, critical linking gaps
**Source agents:** Strategy Lead, Technical SEO Specialist, Web Developer Analyst

**Critical issues (actively harming performance):**
1. **Homepage links to zero blog posts, zero location pages, and only 1 of 6 solution pages** (CRIT-1). This is the single biggest reason Google has not indexed most pages. The homepage is the only page with any external authority, and it passes link equity to only 6 navigation pages and 2 content pages. The remaining 95 pages receive zero internal link equity from the homepage. [Technical SEO, Strategy Lead, UX/CRO, Web Developer -- all 4 agents flagged this]
2. **8 location pages completely orphaned** (CRIT-2). No cross-links between each other, no links from homepage or pillar page. [Technical SEO, Strategy Lead, UX/CRO]
3. **Naked domain 307 redirect** (CRIT-3). orangejelly.co.uk redirects to www.orangejelly.co.uk via 307 (temporary) instead of 301 (permanent), diluting any backlink equity to the naked domain. [Technical SEO]
4. **Sitemap lastModified dates misleading** (CRIT-4). All 39 static pages share identical build-time timestamps, teaching Google to distrust the sitemap signal. [Technical SEO, Strategy Lead]
5. **No analytics data being collected** (CRIT-5). GTM ID is a placeholder. No GA4 property. No Search Console verified. Cookie consent does not gate GTM loading (GDPR risk). [Analytics Specialist]

**Key opportunities:**
- Blog posts already use correct dates from frontmatter
- All pages have proper canonical URLs, meta robots, and HTTPS
- Core Web Vitals likely good (Next.js SSR, image optimisation, font swap)
- Strong security headers in place

**Source agents:** UX/CRO Specialist

**Conversion Effectiveness Score: 19/50 (38%)**

**Key conversion issues:**

2. **Homepage value proposition is abstract** -- H1 says "Transformational hospitality marketing. Built to accelerate growth." A struggling licensee needs to see "I help pubs fill empty tables" within 2 seconds.
3. **High-intent pages are dead ends** -- fix-my-pub, empty-pub-solutions, and location pages have minimal internal links. Visitors who are not ready to WhatsApp have nowhere to go.
4. **No external client social proof** -- all results reference The Anchor. One testimonial from the first external client (September 2025) would dramatically increase trust.
5. **Mobile sticky CTA only on blog posts** -- solution pages and location pages lack the proven StickyCTA component despite being long-scroll pages.

### Content Quality
**Editorial assessment:** Genuinely excellent content, consistency problems
**Source agents:** Editor/QA Lead

**Critical quality issues:**
1. **Quiz night metric inconsistency** -- CLAUDE.md says "25-35 regulars" but 10+ live files say "25-30 teams." Also a unit mismatch: "regulars" vs "teams" are different measurements. [Needs Peter's decision]
2. **Food GP percentages published despite claims audit prohibition** -- The Claims Audit explicitly says "Our food GP should never be mentioned explicitly" yet "58% to 71%" appears in 6 live files including the pub-marketing-agency meta description. [Needs Peter's decision on replacement wording]

5. **Three hidden content sections on quiet-midweek-solutions** -- FAQ section, Midweek Momentum System, and Implementation Timeline are disabled with `{false && ...}`. The FAQ schema still renders, creating a structured data violation (Google sees FAQs that users cannot).

## Ongoing Operating Model

### Sprint Cycle (2-Week Sprints After Initial Implementation)

1. **Review data and opportunities** (Peter + analytics) -- ranking shifts, near-page-one keywords (positions 4-12), traffic changes, crawl errors, competitor movement
2. **Prioritise backlog** -- what moves into next sprint based on impact, effort, and dependencies
3. **Break into execution tracks** -- technical fixes, content optimisation, new content, internal linking, authority building
4. **Create briefs** -- every content piece follows the content flow (opportunity > intent mapping > SERP review > brief > draft > SEO review > editorial QA > publish > post-launch review)
5. **Execute** -- development work, content creation, outreach
6. **QA and sign-off** -- editorial QA for content, technical QA for code changes
7. **Publish/deploy** -- with metadata, schema, links, and tracking confirmed
8. **Measure and learn** -- what improved, what did not, what is close to breaking through
9. **Feed results back into strategy** -- adjust priorities for next sprint

### Meeting Rhythm

| Meeting | Frequency | Who | Purpose |
|---------|-----------|-----|---------|
| Performance + priority review | Weekly (15 min) | Peter | Review GSC/GA4 data, unblock work |
| Content check-in | Weekly (15 min) | Peter (if delegating content) | Status on content creation pipeline |
| Strategic review | Monthly (1 hr) | Peter + Billy | Assess trends, roadmap progress, priority shifts |
| Deep review | Quarterly (2 hrs) | Peter + Billy | Full KPI review, competitor analysis, strategy adjustment |

### Content Production Flow

**For every new blog post or page optimisation:**

1. Opportunity identified (from keyword gaps, SERP analysis, or competitor monitoring)
2. Intent and keyword mapping (primary intent, keyword cluster, commercial value)
3. SERP review (what currently ranks, format, depth, gaps to exploit)
4. Brief creation (purpose, target user, keywords, questions to answer, conversion goal, internal links, metadata)
5. Draft (matching the brief exactly)
6. SEO review (intent match, heading structure, topical completeness, metadata, internal links, cannibalisation check)
7. Editorial QA (clarity, accuracy, factual claims against approved metrics, brand voice, UK English)
8. Publish (correct URL, metadata, schema, internal links, tracking)
9. Post-launch monitoring (indexed? ranking? CTR? cannibalisation?)

### Technical Maintenance Flow

**For every technical SEO change:**

1. Issue identified (from audit, GSC, analytics, or monitoring)
2. Root cause analysis (do not jump to a fix)
3. Impact assessment (how many pages affected, revenue-critical?)
4. Solution design (template-level fix vs page-by-page, risks of the fix itself)
5. Requirement specification (summary, root cause, fix, acceptance criteria, affected areas)
6. Implementation
7. QA (rendered output matches expectations, canonical/robots/schema correct, pages load correctly)
8. Monitoring (crawl behaviour, indexation, rankings, Console errors)

### Handoff Standards

- **Strategy to specialist:** Clear priority, scope, success criteria, deadline, dependencies
- **Specialist to QA:** Completed work matching brief, self-assessment against acceptance criteria, notes on deviations
- **QA to publish:** Confirmed sign-off, conditions/caveats, post-launch monitoring requirements
