## Brand essence

Orange Jelly is a disruptive hospitality growth partner for venues that are ready to stop guessing and start growing.

We are not here to "do a few social posts". We are not here to make pretty websites that sit there doing nothing. We are not here to dress up weak strategy with nice words.

We exist to help hospitality venues become more visible, more bookable, more profitable and more loved by their customers.

Our strength comes from real experience. We do not just advise hospitality businesses from the outside. We run a venue ourselves. We understand the pressure of quiet nights, no-shows, staff costs, food waste, inconsistent bookings, awkward customer conversations, last-minute chaos and the constant need to bring people through the door.

That gives us empathy. It also gives us edge.

We lead with vision, energy and practical action. We believe hospitality businesses deserve better than generic marketing. They deserve strategic thinking, powerful storytelling, intelligent systems and commercially focused activation that actually moves the business forward.

Orange Jelly is bold, passionate, human and commercially sharp.

We help venues build momentum.

## Core positioning statement

Orange Jelly helps hospitality venues get found, get booked and grow through transformative digital strategy, disruptive thinking and real-world operational experience.

We combine AI-supported website strategy, local search, conversion-led content, booking journeys, review generation, event activation and hospitality insight to create growth systems that work in the real world.

## What we believe

### We believe hospitality deserves better marketing

Too many hospitality venues are sold surface-level support: a few posts, a basic website, a menu upload, a Canva graphic and a vague promise of "more awareness".

That is not enough.

Hospitality marketing should connect directly to bookings, footfall, customer experience, revenue and reputation. It should help a venue become easier to find, easier to choose, easier to book and easier to love.

### We believe strategy should be felt, not just seen

A good strategy does not sit in a document. It shows up in the way customers find you, the way they book, the way your team prepares, the way your events fill, the way reviews improve and the way your business starts to feel more in control.

Our work should create momentum people can feel.

### We believe emotion and commercial performance belong together

People choose hospitality venues emotionally. They want warmth, trust, atmosphere, belonging, excitement, comfort, fun and reassurance.

But venues survive commercially. They need bookings, spend, repeat visits, margin and operational control.

Orange Jelly brings both together. We use storytelling to create desire, then build the systems that turn that desire into action.

### We believe lived experience matters

We know the difference between ideas that sound good and ideas that work during live service.

We have tested our thinking in a real pub, with real customers, real staff, real costs and real consequences.

That makes our approach more grounded, more practical and more honest.

### We believe in leading from the front

We do not sit back and wait for growth to happen.

We build the vision, create the plan, test the idea, improve the journey, sharpen the message and keep pushing until the business moves.

Our tone should feel like leadership: clear, energised, encouraging and decisive.

## Tone principles

## 1. Lead with conviction

We should sound certain about what we believe.

Not arrogant. Not overblown. But clear, bold and confident.

Weak:

> We can help with your hospitality marketing.

Stronger:

> We help hospitality venues turn visibility into bookings, bookings into revenue and customers into regulars.

Weak:

> We offer website and social media support.

Stronger:

> We build growth systems for hospitality venues that need more than another pretty website and a few social posts.

## 2. Make it emotional before making it technical

Hospitality is emotional. Our language should connect with the real feelings behind running a venue: pressure, pride, frustration, hope, ambition and relief.

Weak:

> We improve digital customer journeys.

Stronger:

> We help remove the friction, missed opportunities and quiet-night anxiety that hold great venues back.

Weak:

> We support event marketing.

Stronger:

> We help turn empty spaces and quiet sessions into nights people talk about, return to and bring their friends to.

## 3. Always connect story to commercial impact

We can be emotive, but we must never become fluffy. Every big claim should link back to action or outcome.

Example:

> A full room feels good. A full room that books again, leaves a review, brings friends and comes back for food is where the real growth happens.

Example:

> We do not chase likes for the sake of it. We build visibility that leads somewhere: a booking, an enquiry, a visit, a review or a repeat customer.

## 4. Use powerful words, but earn them

Words like transformative, disruptive and industry-leading should be used with proof, context and confidence.

Do not use them as empty slogans.

Weak:

> We are a disruptive, transformative, industry-leading agency.

Stronger:

> We bring a disruptive approach to hospitality growth, replacing guesswork and generic marketing with strategy proven in a real venue.

Stronger:

> Our work is transformative because it changes more than your website. It changes how customers find you, book you, experience you and come back to you.

Stronger:

> We bring industry-leading thinking to independent hospitality venues, combining AI-supported strategy with practical, real-world hospitality experience.

## 5. Speak like a human who understands the room

Our tone should feel personal, direct and alive.

Avoid stiff phrases like:

* We utilise digital solutions
* We provide bespoke marketing services
* We assist clients in maximising engagement
* We leverage multi-channel methodologies
* Our team delivers comprehensive marketing outputs

Use:

* We help venues get found
* We make booking easier
* We turn interest into action
* We build systems that support real growth
* We know what quiet nights feel like
* We know how hard hospitality can be
* We know the difference between noise and momentum

## 6. Be challenging, but constructive

Orange Jelly is disruptive, so the tone should challenge old thinking.

But it should not shame venue owners. Many hospitality businesses are stretched, tired and doing their best.

Good challenger tone:

> If your website is just a digital brochure, it is not working hard enough.

Good challenger tone:

> Posting regularly is not a strategy. A strategy connects visibility, content, bookings, reviews and repeat visits.

Avoid:

> Most venues are doing marketing wrong.

Better:

> Most venues have been sold marketing that is too shallow for the pressure they are under.

## 7. Make the reader feel seen

We should write in a way that makes hospitality operators think, "They get it."

Example:

> We understand the pressure of putting entertainment on and wondering whether anyone will turn up.

Example:

> We know how frustrating it is when the phone rings during service, the diary is unclear, the team are stretched and customers still expect everything to feel effortless.

Example:

> We know that one quiet weekend can knock confidence, and one brilliant event can change the whole mood of the business.

## 8. Keep the energy high

Orange Jelly should feel optimistic, energised and forward-moving.

Use active language:

* Build
* Grow
* Fill
* Shift
* Turn
* Create
* Lead
* Drive
* Spark
* Unlock
* Transform
* Convert
* Strengthen
* Accelerate

Avoid passive, flat language:

* Support
* Assist
* Provide
* Maintain
* Facilitate
* Help with

"Help" is fine sometimes, but the brand should mostly sound more active than that.

## Language rules

## Use "we" with confidence

Orange Jelly should speak as a partner and leader.

Use:

> We build.
> We lead.
> We create.
> We transform.
> We know.
> We have proven.

Avoid:

> We aim to.
> We try to.
> We hope to.
> We may be able to.

## Use "you" to make it personal

Speak directly to venue owners and operators.

Example:

> You do not need more noise. You need a clearer route from customer interest to customer action.

Example:

> Your venue has more potential than your current digital presence is showing.

## Use plain English

Big ideas should still be easy to understand.

Avoid:

> We leverage AI-enabled omnichannel conversion frameworks.

Use:

> We use AI-supported strategy to understand what customers are searching for, then build the pages, journeys and content that help them book.

## Use proof after passion

A strong Orange Jelly paragraph often follows this shape:

1. Emotional truth
2. Challenging insight
3. Strategic answer
4. Proof or outcome

Example:

> A quiet room is not just a quiet room. It is lost revenue, wasted staff time and a knock to confidence. We help venues change that by building connected growth systems across search, bookings, events and customer follow-up. In our own venue, that approach helped grow Google Search visibility by 828%, increase table bookings by 403% and cut no-shows by 89%.

## How we talk about AI

AI should feel like an advantage, not the whole story.

We are not selling AI for the sake of AI. We are using it intelligently to sharpen strategy, improve content, understand customer intent, speed up delivery and create more effective growth systems.

Use:

> AI-supported strategy
> AI-driven insight
> Intelligent content planning
> Search intent mapping
> Faster strategic activation
> Smarter hospitality growth

Avoid:

> Fully automated marketing
> AI will do everything
> Revolutionary AI tools
> Let AI run your venue
> AI-generated content at scale

Example:

> We use AI to move faster and think sharper, but the strategy is grounded in real hospitality experience.

## How we talk about websites

A website is not a brochure. It is a commercial engine.

Use:

> Your website should work like a growth system, not a static menu board.

Example:

> We build websites around what customers are actually searching for, then create clear journeys that turn interest into bookings, enquiries and visits.

Example:

> A beautiful website is not enough. It needs to be findable, persuasive and built around the actions that grow your venue.

## How we talk about social media

We should be clear that Orange Jelly is not just a social posting partner.

Use:

> Social should not be noise. It should be part of a wider growth system.

Example:

> We do not create content just to keep the feed busy. We use content to support events, food, bookings, reputation and repeat visits.

Example:

> Posting is easy. Building momentum is the work.

## How we talk about events

Events are a commercial and emotional growth tool.

Example:

> The best events do more than fill a slot in the calendar. They give people a reason to choose you, talk about you and come back.

Example:

> We design event strategies that create atmosphere, drive spend and build repeat attendance.

Example:

> Entertainment is what happens on the night. Strategy is what makes people turn up, stay, spend, return and bring others with them.

## How we talk about proof

Proof should be specific, confident and simple.

Use:

> Proven in our own venue.
> Tested in live hospitality.
> Built from real trading experience.
> Backed by real results.
> Grounded in operational reality.

Example:

> In our own venue, we grew Google Search visibility by 828%, increased table bookings by 403%, cut no-shows by 89%, and grew food revenue by 98% in three months.

## Final tone benchmark

Every important piece of Orange Jelly copy should pass these questions:

1. Does it sound like we understand hospitality from the inside?
2. Does it feel bold enough to separate us from generic agencies?
3. Does it connect emotion to commercial growth?
4. Does it show leadership, not just support?
5. Does it make the reader feel like change is possible?
6. Does it back up powerful claims with proof?
7. Does it make clear that we are not just here to post on social media?
8. Does it create momentum?

If the answer is yes, it sounds like Orange Jelly.
