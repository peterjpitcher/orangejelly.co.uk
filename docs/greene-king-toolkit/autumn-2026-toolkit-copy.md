`[INTRO]`
Autumn isn't one long season — it's a chain of reasons to visit. A pint of cask in September. A coffee for a good cause. A brilliant alcohol-free round in October. A Halloween night that fills the diary. A new glass off the wine list. A pub full of rugby shirts in November.

We run these moments at The Anchor — our pub just outside Staines — and the lesson's always the same: you don't need to do everything. The pubs that win autumn pick two or three moments, plan early, promote simply, and give guests a reason to **book** before everyone drifts into Christmas mode. Start with the ones that fit your pub — and run them properly.

## Five plays to run this autumn

`[PLAY 1 — FEATURED / give this the most space]`
### 1. Put wine front and centre 🍷
Your new Greene King wine brochure lands this summer — so autumn is the moment to make wine easy to choose and easy to sell. Guests buy more confidently when the list is visible, plain-spoken and talked about by the team.
**Do this now:** pick three wines to champion, add plain-English notes ("juicy and easy", "crisp and dry", "big and warming"), put one on social as a weekly "staff pick", and book a **£10 wine flight night** before party season. Easy drinks-day hooks to build around: **National Vodka Day (4 Oct)**, **Gin & Tonic Day (19 Oct)** and **Champagne Weekend (23–24 Oct)**.

`[PLAY 2]`
### 2. Make low and no a proper choice — Sober October 🍺
No and low isn't a niche any more — **45% of drinkers had a no- or low-alcohol drink in the last year** (Drinkaware, 2025). Sober October is the perfect reason to give drivers, moderators and midweek guests a reason to stay longer.
**Do this now:** build a small low/no board (a beer, a fizz, a mocktail), pair them with food, and brief the team to offer them naturally — *"one of those, or a soft one for the drive?"*

`[PLAY 3]`
### 3. Cask and community in September 🍻
**Cask Ale Week (17–27 Sep)** is the easy excuse to get regulars trying something new, and **Macmillan's Coffee Morning (Fri 25 Sep)** fills a quiet daytime while doing some good.
**Do this now:** chalk up a **Beer of the Day** with a one-line tasting note in plain English, get the team recommending it, and book a Friday-morning coffee-and-cake table for Macmillan.

`[PLAY 4]`
### 4. Own Halloween and Bonfire 🎃
Halloween lands on a **Saturday this year (31 Oct)** — prime trade — with **Bonfire Night the following Thursday (5 Nov)**. It pays off: managed pubs' sales rose almost 2% last October, helped by Halloween (CGA by NIQ).
**Do this now:** split Halloween into a **family afternoon** and a **fancy-dress evening**, open bookings now, and line up a warming Bonfire menu — hot drinks, loaded fries, pies — for the 5th.

`[PLAY 5]`
### 5. Turn late November into Christmas momentum 🏉
November is a rugby month: the new **Nations Championship** runs all month and finishes with a **Finals Weekend at Twickenham (Fri 27–Sun 29 Nov)**. That same weekend brings **Black Friday**, then **Cyber Monday** and **St Andrew's Day** (both Mon 30 Nov).
**Do this now:** take big-screen **table bookings for your nation's games early** — there's a big match most weekends. And rather than discounting, **sell gift cards, take Christmas party deposits, and push a January "bounce-back" voucher**. Add a Scottish special for St Andrew's if it suits your pub.

`[RESPONSIBLE RETAILING BOX — tinted panel]`
> **Keep it responsible.** Challenge 25 on every event, sensible drinks messaging, and Drinkaware where it fits. Running something with fireworks, music, tickets or extended hours? Check your premises licence and whether you need a **Temporary Event Notice** with your council — at least four weeks ahead.
