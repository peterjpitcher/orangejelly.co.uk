## 4. Rugby — fix the wording, then make it a highlight

**What's confirmed (authoritative — World Rugby / Six Nations):**
- The **Nations Championship** is real and **launches in 2026** (inaugural, biennial).
- **Finals Weekend: Twickenham (Allianz Stadium), London, Fri 27 – Sun 29 November 2026.** ← name the venue.
- November pool window for the home nations: **6–21 November**; full window incl. finals: 6–29 November.

**Two corrections:**
1. **"Three fixtures in July, three in November"** → it's three *rounds per window*; each home nation plays **3 matches in November plus a 4th on Finals Weekend**. Reword to avoid implying the whole tournament is three games.
2. **"Autumn Internationals" vs "Nations Championship"** are **not two separate events in 2026** — the traditional standalone Autumn Internationals are *replaced* by the Nations Championship's November (Northern Series) games. Don't list them as two things.

**The upgrade (this is the single biggest strengthening opportunity):** pubs care *which* games are on. Naming the marquee home-nation fixtures turns "plan for rugby" into "here are your bookable Saturdays." Flagship draws for trade:
